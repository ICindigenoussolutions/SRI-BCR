The Sovereignty Gap

Essential dollars.
Essential decisions.
Leaving the community.

Food Sovereignty

Food Dollars Flowing Out

Every grocery purchase sends revenue outside the community. Supply chains are controlled by external parties, leaving the Nation without ownership of the infrastructure that feeds its people.

Energy Sovereignty

Energy Dollars Flowing Out

Power costs represent one of the largest ongoing community expenses. Those dollars leave with each billing cycle, generating no long-term community asset or return.

Workforce Sovereignty

Youth Leaving for Employment

Skilled, motivated community members relocate for career opportunities that do not yet exist locally. Human capital — the Nation's most valuable resource — is lost to external labour markets.

Economic Sovereignty

Limited Asset Ownership

Without ownership of essential infrastructure, economic activity passes through the community without accumulating within it. Revenue circulates outward rather than compounding inward.

True sovereignty requires ownership of essential infrastructure — the systems that feed, power, employ, and sustain a community must be controlled by that community.

The Initiative

What Sovereign Roots
Actually Delivers

Sovereign Roots is not a pilot program or abstract concept. It is a structured infrastructure initiative developed by IC Indigenous Solutions Inc. for deployment on Nation territory — with a clear deliverable at the end of Phase 1: a complete feasibility package that enables Chief & Council to make a fully informed decision.

01

Modular Controlled-Environment Agriculture Facility

A purpose-designed food production system engineered for the Nation's land, climate, and supply requirements. Scalable, energy-efficient, and capable of producing fresh food year-round regardless of geography or season.

02

Resilient Hybrid Energy Plan

An energy infrastructure plan developed specifically for remote and semi-remote conditions — designed to reduce dependence on external utilities, lower long-term costs, and provide reliable power to the facility and community.

03

K–Career Workforce Development Pathway

An integrated education and employment pipeline — from school curriculum to certified technical roles — designed to create long-term workforce sovereignty by developing operators, technicians, and leaders within the community.

04

Governance Structure Under the Nation's EDE

All assets and operations are structured under the Nation's Economic Development Entity. Ownership is vested in the Nation. Governance is independent, professionally managed, and accountable to Chief & Council.

05

Feasibility Package with Clear Go/No-Go Outcome

Phase 1 concludes with a comprehensive feasibility report presented to Chief & Council. Every subsequent phase requires explicit authorization. No decision to proceed is assumed or implied at this stage.

Phase 1 is exploratory. It answers one question: Is this viable for our Nation? Chief & Council decides what happens next.

Governance Sovereignty

Control remains
exactly where it belongs.

  • I

    100% Nation Ownership

    All assets are owned by the Nation and held through the Nation's Economic Development Entity. No equity is transferred to external parties.

  • II

    Independent EDE Structure

    Operations are structured under an independent EDE with a separate governance board — providing commercial discipline without removing political oversight.

  • III

    Professional Management

    The facility is managed by qualified professionals under standards appropriate to the scale and type of operation. Leadership remains accountable to the Nation.

  • IV

    Transparent Reporting

    Regular financial and operational reporting is provided to Chief & Council. Performance against plan is tracked, documented, and disclosed.

  • V

    Political Oversight Preserved

    Chief & Council retains full decision-making authority at every phase. The EDE structure is designed to protect that authority, not circumvent it.

Sovereign Roots is structured to strengthen the Nation's governance capacity. The EDE model creates a professional operational layer that handles day-to-day management decisions — freeing leadership to focus on strategic direction and community priorities.

This structure has been specifically designed to ensure that no outside party can exert control over Nation-owned assets. ICISI acts as architect and advisor. The Nation acts as owner and decision-maker.

Every governance mechanism in this initiative is designed with one objective: to ensure that what is built for the Nation serves the Nation — permanently.

The Resolution

What This Resolution
Authorizes — and Does Not.

A Band Council Resolution to authorize an Exploratory MOU with IC Indigenous Solutions Inc. is a decision to examine the opportunity. It is not a commitment to proceed with construction, capital investment, or any binding obligation. Every substantive decision follows a separate, explicit authorization by Chief & Council.

✓ This Resolution Authorizes
  • ✓ A comprehensive feasibility study conducted by ICISI on behalf of the Nation
  • ✓ A formal assessment of suitable sites on Nation territory
  • ✓ Identification and exploration of available capital and funding pathways
  • ✓ Structured stakeholder discussions with relevant technical and development partners
  • ✓ Delivery of a complete feasibility package to Chief & Council for review and decision
◻ This Resolution Does Not
  • — Commit the Nation to any construction or infrastructure development
  • — Commit any community or Nation capital or financial resources
  • — Transfer or encumber any Nation land or assets
  • — Create debt, guarantee, or financial obligation of any kind
  • — Remove or diminish the authority of Chief & Council at any stage

"Chief & Council retains full decision-making authority at every stage. No phase proceeds without explicit authorization."
